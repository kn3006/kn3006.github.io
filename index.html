Extreme Leadership

Signature Program

The most daunting task for any business leader today is “How to succeed”. Economic uncertainty, market consolidation and the need for growth through innovation have rendered the best of organizations to survival pressures and made it imperative for leaders to manage through multifaceted business challenges.

Dealing with the pressures of leading in the 21st century has its own set of challenges. Leaders now must manage performance in an intensely turbulent and complex, global business environment — one that constantly places new demands on their organizations and the capabilities of the workforce. Increasing competition, market scrutiny and employee expectations now require that leaders not only deliver business results, but that they do so in an inherently authentic, transparent and inclusive manner.

By any measures it’s a tall order for even the highest-performing executives. This then brings us to a point where we need to address the following burning questions:

  • What kind of leadership will be effective in steering organizations to success through such economic volatility and chaos?
  • What are the emerging skills required for such leadership?
  • How can one build and sustain an effective leadership strategy?

Extreme Leadership: The New Imperative
Extreme leadership is a refreshing and practical approach for today’s extreme challenges that acknowledges the complexity and fluid nature of business change. It is profoundly thoughtful and a useful approach to managing today’s chaotic environment.

Extreme leadership workshop is about a new way to understand leadership given the managerial challenges that the world is throwing at us today. Traditional leadership methodologies and practices may only help a little to gain a futile control over chaos, but this training program provides proven approaches for managing the challenges of today.

Entrepreneurial Leadership

Signature Program

Entrepreneurial leadership stems from the notion of a leader acting as being self-employed. It involves instilling the confidence to think, behave and act with an entrepreneurial mindset in the best interests and intended purpose of the organization. An entrepreneurial leader takes on himself the responsibility to create such conditions that instead of being under control the organization generates its own order and responds creatively to the environment.

Surviving in the 21st century entails continuous learning and changing, a very strong characteristic of entrepreneurism. Focusing on the important and taking leadership decisions to manage the business will be the key management function. Unfortunately, in mature companies, more and more employees are overlooking company survival and customers as their objectives and focus on their own personal gain. Such drifting focus just results in poor decisions, less job satisfaction and eluding professional success.

Entrepreneurial Leadership is a workshop exclusively designed to help discover the core strengths of entrepreneurs, their ability to spot gaps, filling them and in the process contributing to society and economy, their behaviors related to strategy and leadership, and learning to apply the entrepreneurial mindset for achieving sustainable competitive advantage and lasting success in an organizational setting.

Leadership and Intellectual Humility

Signature Program

There’s a well-known Indian parable about six blind men who argue at length about what an elephant feels like. Each has a different idea, and each holds fast to his own view. “It’s like a rope,” says the man who touched the tail. “Oh no, it’s more like the solid branch of a tree,” contends the one who touched the trunk. And so on and so forth, and round and round they go.

The moral of the story: We all tend to overestimate how much we know — which, in turn, means that we often cling stubbornly to our beliefs while tuning out opinions different from our own. And in a time when it seems like we’re all more convinced than ever of our own rightness, social scientists have begun to look more closely at an antidote: a concept called Intellectual Humility.

Intellectual humility is the ability of a person who has an accurate assessment of both, his strengths and weaknesses, and views all this in the context of a larger whole and is grounded and liberated by this knowledge. Knowing his abilities, he asks how he can contribute and recognizing his deficiencies he asks how he can grow?

Research indicates that intellectually humble people are more pro-social, build connections, helpful, tolerant, sensitive and open to accepting differences. They have also been seen to behave more ethically and are honest during negotiations. Google’s VP in charge of hiring, Laszlo Bock, has claimed it as one of the top qualities he looks for in a candidate: Without intellectual humility, he has said, “you are unable to learn.”

Leadership and Intellectual Humility is an high impact specially designed workshop that helps develop a state of openness to new ideas, a willingness to be receptive to new sources of evidence, develop superior learners and improve the ability to engage in civil discourse.

Cognitive Flexibility

Signature Program

Rational thought is an analysis that can be described by a kind of an algorithm followed by computers. Flexible thought cannot. As humans we have evolved the capacity of flexible thinking in order to help us face the challenges of everyday life and help us succeed when circumstances change – which is why it is increasingly important to hone these skills today.

Cognitive flexibility can be broadly defined as the ability to shift one’s thinking from old situations to new situations as well as the ability to overcome responses or thinking that have become habitual and adapt to the new situation.

In simple terms, cognitive flexibility is the ability to shift the course of thoughts or actions according to the changing demand of the situation.

Change is inevitable and each one of us has to undergo change at some point in life. Many a times change is perceived as a pain for the simple reason of all the work it may entail. People don’t like change if it harms them, creates undue risk, or causes them to put it extra effort. On the other hand, no one likes repetitive jobs, or watching the same episode on television every night. We humans are designed to prefer little variety if it comes at no extra cost.

Cognitive Flexibility is a new age workshop, exclusively designed to encourage the development of creativity needed for innovation where people use varieties of information and tools to identify and solve problems by creating new useful or imaginative solutions.

Critical Thinking

Corporate Program

Everyone thinks; it is human nature to do so. However, much of human thinking if left to itself, is biased, distorted, partial, uninformed or down-right prejudiced. But still the quality of our life and that of what we produce, make, or build, depends precisely on the quality of our thoughts. It therefore follows that shoddy thinking is costly, at times fatal, both in money and in quality of life.

This calls for systematically cultivating excellence in thoughts. Critical Thinking is that mode of thinking – about any subject, content, or problem – in which the thinker improves the quality of his thinking by skillfully taking charge of the structures inherent in thinking and imposing intellectual standards upon them.

Critical thinking is a self-guided, self-disciplined thinking that attempts to reason at the highest level of quality in a fair-minded way. It refers to the ability to analyze information objectively and make a reasoned and informed judgement, and is therefore one of the most sought-after skills in every industry and every workplace.

Planning and Prioritization

Corporate Program

At work or in life, we spend time in certain ways because we choose to. We have the choice to work, or not to work, to attend to every day menial tasks or not, although are choices are not always deliberate or visible. As humans, we make a choice depending upon the reward received or anticipated. Rewards are what we expect to gain from doing or postponing a task. The bitter truth is “good rewards come from good time management” be it academics, work, business or life.

We operate daily in the tension that the urgent and important bring. The problem is that the “important” tasks seldom must be done today, but the “urgent” tasks call for instant action. Endless demands pressure every hour and every day. As we push the “important” back one more day, we slowly become slaves to the tyranny of the urgent.

Planning and prioritizing are important skills because they help you identify and focus on the order in which things need to be accomplished. Very rarely do you only have one thing on your to-do list! Being able to prioritize what needs to be completed and make a plan to do so are essential for work efficiency and maintaining work-life balance.

Planning and Prioritizing Skills training program is exclusively designed program to help people manage their time better by analyzing goals, breaking goals into tasks, sequencing and queuing in order of importance, setting clear and measurable goals and develop their own highly effective to do list.
